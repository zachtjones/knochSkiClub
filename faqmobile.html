Do I have to know how to ski or board
to join KDA?
No, all the resorts that are visited have
courteous and professional instructors
that will teach you how to safely enjoy
the sport correctly. As a KDA member
your lessons are free. Add all this to the
large group of people with you who are
also learning, and that equals loads of fun.

What if I do not have my own
equipment?
There are rentals available at most resorts
as well as Kenmac Rental (Butler) or Peak
Ski and Board (Gibsonia). KDA members
benefit from group rates and discount
rentals. Beginners and younger members
are actually discouraged from buying
equipment. It is less expensive to rent than
to buy and with the changing technology in
skis and snowboards, the rental equipment
is usually up to date in style and
development. Both local rental shops do
season rentals for those who would like
the flexibility of having equipment, but not
the cost.

How much does a trip cost?
Prices vary depending on destination, but
compared to individual rates, KDA group
rates are a huge savings and include the
safety of transportation on a charter bus.

What if I do not know anyone is KDA?
This is probably one of the best reasons to
join KDA. One of the attributes of the
organization is the friendly, welcoming
atmosphere of a KDA trip. Older/veteran
members remember being new/younger
and seek out new members to help them
and be-friend them. Being a part of a
KDA trip tends to be more of a small
group or camp environment where people
get to know each other.
